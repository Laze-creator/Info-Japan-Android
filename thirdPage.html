What is an anime
  • The term anime is derived from animÄ“shon, the translation of animation into English.
    Anime is both a commercial entertainment product, a mass popular cultural phenomenon and a technological
    art form that is produced for different audiences such as children, teenagers and adults.
    Japan's anime formats are commonly referred to as TV anime (TVA), original video anime (OVA), movie and NET anime (NETA). So basically an anime is an animation of a manga or light novel, therefore a frametisation of drawings made by hand with sometimes the use of cgi,
    which consists of a software that simulates through the computer movements too complex to make by hand.
3
Short history of anime
  • The first anime to be released on television was "Tetsuwan Atom" (Astro Boy) by Osamu Tezuka in 1963 and lasted 193 episodes of 30 minutes each.
    It was after the first half of the sixties that the animation industry boomed: production studios multiplied,
    techniques became more and more refined, and private television stations, as well as the state television NHK, progressively increased their demand for animated series.
    In addition, from the beginning and more and more often, anime characters were exploited for advertising purposes, followed by the birth of merchandising gadgets and models.
